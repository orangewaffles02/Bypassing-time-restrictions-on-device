# Bypassing-time-restrictions-on-device
Extremely simple guide on bypassing downtime limit (e.g. forced shutdown at 11 p.m.) by changing the device's native time.

<h2>Extremely simple followable guide</h2>
<p>Say your device auto shutdowns after 10:30pm due to some restrictions set by your administrator. Don't like that? Here is a simple solution.</p>
<p>To change your devices native time, we first need to access your device's BIOS settings. Hopefully it isnt locked for u or you're kinda cooked. BIOS settings can be accessed in a number of ways. The simplest way is to search up your device's BIOS settings key and spam it while your computer is booting up. If that isn't working, use this surefire way to access BIOS on any windows device.</p>
<p>Click the windows icon at the middle of the taskbar(next to search bar). Once you have opened up start button, look for the power button at the bottom left of the window. Click it and you should see "power off" and "restart". <b>While holding shift on your keyboard,</b> click "restart"</p>
<p>This will bring you into a blue screen upon restarting. Select "troubleshoot"-> "UEFI Firmware settings" -> "restart"</p>
<p>After restarting, this will bring you into the BIOS screen.</p>
<p>In the BIOS screen, you are already 80% the way there. Navigate to date and time, and change it to a value that isnt locked. Do not touch anyting else. When you're done, Save and Exit. </p>
This should change your devices native time, allowing you to bypass any time restrictions set on your Windows device.
